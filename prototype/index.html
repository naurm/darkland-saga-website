The Pillar of Light

Just beyond the southern mountains of Ciallmhar, the Pillar stretches toward the sky height unknown — the only thing keeping the Storm and the Bane from finding a permanent home in Arsatir. Clouds swirl around the mile-wide beacon.

No one fully understands the ancient magic that keeps it lit. Every year a ritual involving all four nations recharges its light. For those on the borders, the hours feel like days while the ritual is completed.

When the Pillar’s light shines again, bane that have slipped across the border slink into the long shadows its light casts, hungering for their chance to turn creatures of light to darkness.— Anonymous border keeper, Ciallmhar Garrison
Recorded in the seventh year of the Renewal

The Storm and the Bane

The Storm is a dominating force of darkness over Eadrom. Clouds of pure black form its vanguard, its wall stretching as far as the eye can see beyond surveyed territory — bunching against the border of Arsatir, demanding entry.

Bane are ill-made creatures sent beyond the borders under cover of the Storm. Most are wild animals corrupted by other bane. Infected humans die of the corruption if not treated quickly. Bane cannot survive long in the light.

Hellcat

A large, broad-shouldered black cat whose body is covered in small hard coin-sized interlocking plates. When not stalking prey, it raises these plates to let the flames beneath rage. Its pupil glows like a living ember.

Hellcats are highly intelligent and resistant to bane corruption. A mixture of fire and darkness, they hunt and feed on bane — the inferno within burns away corruption.

Due to their solitary nature, forming a bond is no recorded instances.

The World

Arsatir and beyond

What follows is not a compendium. It is an evocation — sketches of a world drawn from the stories told within it. More exists than is recorded here. That is by design.

Territorial sketch
Geography

The Four Nations

Ciallmhar — Northeast. The Smouldering Forest, where rivers of lava run beneath trees that crack with heat. The Crown grants nobility to banehunters who distinguish themselves.

Oroa — Northwest. The Fallen Mountains. The tallest people of Arsatir, with longer lives and muscular builds.

Proteya — West. Coastal nation. At the center of a maelstrom location disputed lies dry land where waterstones can be mined.

The Wildwood — East to Southeast. Great forest spanning from mid-continent to the southern tip. Home to griffon riders and forest drakes.

Cultural survey
Peoples

The banehunters

In Ciallmhar, the Crown incentivizes the hunting of bane through the granting of nobility and lands to houses that impress in the banehunter guild’s rankings. Despite this, the banehunter nobility is not viewed as true nobility by the historical houses.

A banehunter’s life is solitary by necessity. The work demands it. Bonds form slowly, but they form deeply—between hunters, between a hunter and a town that becomes home, between a hunter and a hellcat that chooses to walk beside them.

Arcane study
Magic

Essence and weaving

Essence stones are found at locations of extreme elemental intensity—deep earth, coastal maelstroms, volcanic vents, high wind passes. Bonding with a stone is not a transaction; it is a relationship.

Earth demands a desire to make whole. Wind demands openness and release. Fire demands precision and discipline. Water requires trust.

Those who master multiple elements are rare. Using more than one in combat is said to be exceptionally difficult, though no living practitioner verified.

There is a fifth tradition, spoken of only in fragments: darkstones.

Storyteller

From the keeper’s hand

The Darkland Saga was not built from outlines and worldbuilding documents. It was discovered—unearthed slowly, over years of writing, in the spaces between scenes, in the questions characters asked that their author could not yet answer.

This world began with a single image: a pillar of light at dusk, a border wall, and someone standing at the edge of the known, choosing to step forward rather than back.

The themes that emerged—endurance, stewardship, light that persists not because it dazzles but because it holds—were not planned. They were found, embedded in the story like fossils waiting to be uncovered.

To write a world is not to control it. It is to learn humility before something larger than yourself. The Darkland Saga taught me that.

On themes

Light that holds

The saga is not about heroic triumph over darkness. It is about ordinary faithfulness standing against overwhelming odds. The courage of people who are not special—except in their refusal to abandon each other.

On craft

Writing the threshold

Every scene in the Darkland Saga is a threshold. Someone is about to cross into unknown territory—geographic, emotional, spiritual. The craft question is always: what do they carry with them, and what must they leave behind?

On inspiration

What lights the ember

The landscapes of the Darkland Saga are drawn from real places: the Scottish Highlands at dusk, the Pacific Northwest in winter, the English moors under heavy cloud. But the soul of the world was shaped by older things—myth, faith, and the question of what makes people endure what they cannot escape.
